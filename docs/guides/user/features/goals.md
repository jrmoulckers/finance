# Goal Tracking

Goals give your money a destination. Whether you're saving for an emergency fund, a vacation, a house down payment, or a new laptop, Finance helps you track your progress, see projections, and stay motivated.

## What is a goal?

A goal is a savings target with an optional deadline. It tracks how much you've saved toward something specific, shows your progress as a percentage, and — if you set a deadline — tells you whether you're on track.

Goals are separate from your budget. Your budget allocates monthly spending; goals track progress toward a longer-term target.

### Example goals

| Goal                | Target     | Deadline     |
| ------------------- | ---------- | ------------ |
| Emergency Fund      | $10,000    | None         |
| Summer Vacation     | $3,000     | June 2026    |
| House Down Payment  | $50,000    | December 2028|
| New Laptop          | $2,000     | March 2026   |

---

## Creating a goal

1. Go to **More → Goals**.
2. Tap **Add Goal** (the + button).
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Name** — a descriptive name (e.g., "House Down Payment").
   - **Target amount** — how much you're saving toward.
   - **Deadline** (optional) — when you want to reach the goal. If set, Finance calculates how much you need to save each month.
   - **Icon and color** — customize the appearance for easy identification.
4. Tap **Save**.

Your new goal appears on the Goals screen with a progress bar starting at 0%.

### Goal detail screen

Tap a goal to see its full detail view:

- **Progress visualization** — a larger progress bar or chart
- **Contribution history** — a list of every contribution with dates and amounts
- **Monthly breakdown** — how much you've contributed each month
- **Projection** — when you'll reach the goal at your current pace

## Funding a goal

To put money toward a goal, you make a **contribution**.

### Steps

1. Open the goal (tap it on the Goals screen).
2. Tap **Add Contribution**.
3. Enter the **amount** you're contributing.
4. Optionally add a **note** (e.g., "Tax refund" or "Monthly savings").
5. Tap **Save**.

The goal's progress bar and remaining amount update immediately.

### Where does the contribution come from?

Contributions interact with your budget:

- **From "To Budget"** — if you have unbudgeted income, you can assign it directly to a goal. This decreases your "To Budget" amount.
- **From a budget category** — if you have a "Savings" budget category, contributions to goals reduce that category's remaining balance.
- **Manual tracking** — you can also record a contribution as a standalone action to simply track progress without connecting it to a budget category.

### Reversing a contribution

Made a mistake? On the goal's contribution history, tap the contribution and select **Delete**. The goal's progress adjusts accordingly.

## Projections and pacing

If you set a deadline on a goal, Finance calculates projections to help you stay on track.

### Required monthly amount

Finance divides the remaining amount by the number of months until the deadline:

```
Monthly target = (Target − Current) ÷ Months remaining
```

For example, if you need $26,500 more and you have 24 months: $26,500 ÷ 24 = **$1,104.17/month**.

### Pace indicator

Based on your recent contribution history, Finance shows whether you're:

| Status      | Meaning                                                   |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **On track**| Your average monthly contributions meet or exceed the required pace |
| **Ahead**   | You're saving faster than needed — you'll reach the goal early     |
| **Behind**  | Your recent contributions are below the required pace              |

### Projected completion date

Even if you don't set a deadline, Finance calculates when you'll reach the goal based on your average monthly contributions:

> "At this pace, you'll reach your goal by **March 2028**."

If your pace changes, the projection updates automatically.

## Milestones and celebrations

Finance celebrates your progress at key milestones:

| Milestone | What happens                                               |
| --------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **25%**   | "Quarter of the way there!" — a small visual celebration   |
| **50%**   | "Halfway! Keep going." — a milestone card on the dashboard |
| **75%**   | "Almost there!" — an encouraging nudge                     |
| **100%**  | "You did it! 🎉" — the goal is marked complete            |

Milestone celebrations are intentionally low-key and encouraging. They acknowledge your progress without being over-the-top or disruptive.

> ♿ On platforms that support it, milestones include gentle haptic feedback. If you have reduced motion enabled, animations are suppressed and milestones are shown as static messages.

## Editing and completing goals

### Editing a goal

You can change a goal's name, target amount, deadline, icon, or color at any time:

1. Open the goal.
2. Tap **Edit** (pencil icon).
3. Make your changes.
4. Tap **Save**.

Changing the target amount recalculates your progress percentage and pacing instantly.

### Completing a goal

When you reach 100%, Finance marks the goal as **completed** automatically. Completed goals move to a "Completed" section on the Goals screen.

You can also manually mark a goal as complete if you've reached it outside the app.

### Deleting a goal

If you decide to abandon a goal:

1. Open the goal.
2. Tap **Delete**.
3. Confirm.

The goal and its contribution history are removed. This does not affect your account balances or budget — contributions you made are still in your transaction history.

## Multiple goals

You can have as many active goals as you want. The Goals screen shows them all with their individual progress.

### Prioritizing goals

Finance lists goals in the order you created them, but you can reorder them to reflect your priorities. Drag to reorder, or use the sort options:

- By deadline (soonest first)
- By progress (closest to completion first)
- By amount remaining (smallest first)
- Custom order (drag to reorder)

### Balancing goals with your budget

A practical approach to funding multiple goals:

1. Include a "Savings" category in your monthly budget.
2. When you have money to save, distribute it across goals based on priority.
3. Use the required monthly amounts as a guide — fund the most time-sensitive goals first.

## Tips and best practices

1. **Start with one goal.** Pick the one that matters most to you right now — an emergency fund is a common first goal. Once you've built the savings habit, add more.

2. **Set realistic targets.** If you earn $4,000/month and your expenses are $3,500, setting a $500/month savings goal is achievable. Setting $2,000/month is not — and missing it feels discouraging.

3. **Deadlines create urgency.** A goal without a deadline is just a wish. Set a date, even if it's approximate, and Finance gives you a monthly target to hit.

4. **Celebrate milestones.** Take a moment at 25%, 50%, and 75% to acknowledge your progress. Saving money is hard, and you're doing it.

5. **Adjust, don't abandon.** If your circumstances change, edit the target or deadline rather than deleting the goal. Progress toward a modified goal is still progress.

6. **Link goals to purpose.** Name your goals with emotion: "Dream Vacation to Japan" hits differently than "Savings Goal 2." When the purpose is clear, the motivation follows.

7. **Use the projection.** The "At this pace, you'll reach your goal by…" message is your most powerful motivator. Check it regularly.
