## 2. Core Narrative Arc

### The Story We Tell

**v1.0 promise:** "Your finances, your device, your control."
**v2.0 evolution:** "Now with the insight to act on it."

v1.0 proved that a privacy-first budget tracker could be genuinely useful. v2.0 answers the next question users asked: "Okay, I can see my money — now help me understand it."

### Three-Act Structure

1. **Act 1 — Foundation (v1.0):** We built a private, offline-first budget tracker that works on every platform. Users trusted us with their financial data because we never asked to see it.
2. **Act 2 — Intelligence (v2.0):** Now we add the intelligence layer — health scores, reports, widgets, and protected categories — without compromising on privacy. Every computation happens on your device.
3. **Act 3 — Empowerment (ongoing):** You don't just track spending anymore. You understand your financial health, build custom reports, and keep your most sensitive spending truly private. Finance grows with you.

### Key Tension to Resolve

> "How can a privacy-first app add 'smart' features without compromising on privacy?"

**Answer:** All v2.0 intelligence runs on-device. The Financial Health Score is calculated locally. Reports are generated locally. Widgets read from your local database. Biometric categories use your device's secure hardware. No data leaves your device for any v2.0 feature.

## 3. Key Messages by Audience

### Privacy-Conscious Users

**Primary message:** "v2.0 adds intelligence without compromising privacy. Every new feature runs entirely on your device."

**Supporting points:**

- Financial Health Score computed locally from your own data
- Biometric-protected categories use Face ID / fingerprint / Windows Hello — no server
- Custom reports generated on-device, exported locally
- Widgets read from your local encrypted database
- Source-available code means you can verify every claim

### Budget-Conscious Users (YNAB Switchers)

**Primary message:** "The budgeting power you need, now with financial health tracking — and it's still free at the core."

**Supporting points:**

- Financial Health Score gives you a 0–100 view of where you stand
- Custom report builder creates the views YNAB can't
- Home screen widgets keep your budget top of mind
- No $14.99/month subscription needed for core features

### ADHD / Neurodivergent Community

**Primary message:** "Finance v2.0 makes your financial picture even easier to absorb — at a glance, on your terms."

**Supporting points:**

- Widgets show your key numbers without opening the app
- Health Score is one number — no information overload

- Report builder lets you see exactly what you want, nothing else

### Technical / Developer Audience

**Primary message:** "On-device AI, cross-platform widgets, biometric APIs, and a report engine — all built with KMP."

**Supporting points:**

- Health Score algorithm is deterministic, reproducible, documented
- Biometric APIs: Face ID, BiometricPrompt, Windows Hello, WebAuthn
- Widget frameworks: WidgetKit, Glance, Adaptive Cards, PWA tiles
- Report engine generates PDFs on-device across all platforms
- Source-available — read the implementation yourself

### Families / Households

**Primary message:** "Budget together without seeing everything. Protected categories keep personal spending private, even in shared budgets."

**Supporting points:**

- Biometric-protected categories are per-person in shared households
- Collaborative budget negotiation lets everyone have a voice
- Shared budgets, private spending — the balance families need
- Family plan includes all v2.0 features for every household member

## 7. Messaging Do / Don't

### Do

- ✅ Emphasize that all v2.0 intelligence runs on-device
- ✅ Use specific technical terms (Face ID, AES-256, on-device) — specificity builds trust
- ✅ Acknowledge that some features are premium — be transparent about tiers
- ✅ Celebrate user progress ("See how far you've come")
- ✅ Use inclusive language — health scores are informational, not judgmental
- ✅ Reference v1.0 foundation — v2.0 builds on a trusted base

### Don't

- ❌ Imply that users with low health scores are failing — present it as a starting point
- ❌ Use fear-based messaging about competitors' privacy practices
- ❌ Overstate AI capabilities — health scores are formula-based, not AI
- ❌ Create artificial urgency around the upgrade
- ❌ Suggest biometric categories are only for "hiding" spending — frame as privacy and autonomy
- ❌ Compare health scores between users in marketing — benchmarking is opt-in and private

## 8. Privacy Claims Update for v2.0

### New Claims (Must Be Technically Verified)

| Marketing Claim                                             | Technical Basis                                                                                | Verification Required                          |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| "Health Score calculated on your device"                    | KMP `commonMain` score algorithm, no network call                                              | ✅ Verify no server dependency                 |
| "Biometric lock uses Face ID / fingerprint / Windows Hello" | Platform-native biometric APIs (LocalAuthentication, BiometricPrompt, Windows Hello, WebAuthn) | ✅ Verify each platform impl                   |
| "Widgets read from local encrypted database"                | WidgetKit/Glance/Adaptive Cards read SQLCipher DB                                              | ✅ Verify no network fetch in widget providers |
| "Reports generated on-device"                               | PDF generation in KMP/platform code                                                            | ✅ Verify no server-side rendering             |
| "Benchmarking uses differential privacy"                    | k-anonymity (min 50), noise injection in Edge Function                                         | ✅ Verify implementation matches claim         |

### Updated Privacy Card (v2.0)

> **Your money stays on your device.** Finance v2.0 stores all your financial data in an encrypted database on your device using AES-256 encryption (SQLCipher). Your Financial Health Score, custom reports, and widget data are all computed and stored locally. Biometric-protected categories use your device's secure hardware — Face ID, fingerprint, or Windows Hello. No data is uploaded to any server unless you choose to enable multi-device sync. The codebase is source-available — verify every claim at github.com/jrmoulckers/finance.
