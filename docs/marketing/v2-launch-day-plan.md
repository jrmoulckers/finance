## 1. Launch Day Overview

### Launch Objective

Ship Finance v2.0 to all 4 platforms simultaneously, drive maximum awareness through coordinated press, social, and community outreach, and transition beta testers smoothly to the production release.

### Success Criteria (Launch Day)

| Metric                         | Target           | Measurement      |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------- | ---------------- |
| All 4 platform releases live   | Yes              | Store status     |
| Product Hunt: Top 5 of the day | Yes              | Product Hunt     |
| Social media impressions       | 20K+             | Social analytics |
| App store page views           | 2x daily average | Store analytics  |
| Downloads (launch day)         | 3x daily average | Store analytics  |
| Critical bugs reported         | 0                | Support channels |
| Press coverage published       | 2+ articles      | Media monitoring |

### Launch Day Principles

- **Coordinate, don't overwhelm.** Stagger posts across channels. Don't flood.
- **Be human.** Launch posts should feel genuine, not corporate. Personal stories over press-speak.
- **Engage, don't broadcast.** Respond to every comment and question personally.
- **Stay honest.** If something goes wrong, acknowledge it immediately and transparently.
- **Celebrate the community.** Credit beta testers, contributors, and early supporters.

## 5. Contingency Plans

### Scenario: App Store Rejection (iOS or Windows)

**Trigger:** Store review rejects v2.0 build.

**Response:**

1. Launch on approved platforms (Android, Web) on schedule
2. Social messaging: "Finance v2.0 is live on Android and Web. iOS/Windows coming shortly — working through store review."
3. Address rejection reason immediately (usually privacy labels or metadata)
4. Resubmit with fixes; expedite review if possible
5. Update launch posts when remaining platforms are approved

### Scenario: Critical Bug Discovered on Launch Day

**Trigger:** Bug affecting >5% of users or data integrity issue.

**Response:**

1. If data-loss risk: pull the update immediately, revert to v1.x
2. If UI/UX issue: post acknowledgment on social, fix and push hotfix
3. Social: "We found a bug in v2.0 that affects [description]. We're fixing it now. Your data is safe. Update coming within [timeframe]."
4. Never minimize or ignore — transparency builds trust
5. Post-mortem after resolution

### Scenario: Product Hunt Underperforms

**Trigger:** Not trending toward top 5 by midday.

**Response:**

1. Don't panic — Product Hunt is one channel, not the only channel
2. Focus engagement on HN, Reddit, and social where momentum may be stronger
3. Do NOT ask friends/team to upvote — Product Hunt penalizes this
4. Post additional genuine comments on the Product Hunt page
5. Accept the result gracefully — some launches are slow burns

### Scenario: Negative Press or Social Backlash

**Trigger:** Significant negative reaction (privacy concern, pricing complaint, etc.).

**Response:**

1. Read and understand the criticism before responding
2. If factually wrong: correct gently with specific evidence (link to source code)
3. If valid concern: acknowledge, thank for feedback, describe plan to address
4. Never argue, deflect, or delete criticism
5. If privacy claim is questioned: link to architecture docs and source code

### Scenario: Server Issues (Sync/Backend)

**Trigger:** Backend outage affecting sync users.

**Response:**

1. Reassure users: "Finance works offline. Your local data is unaffected."
2. Post status update on social and GitHub Discussions
3. Fix server issue (DevOps)
4. Post resolution update
5. Reminder in messaging: "Finance is designed to work without a server. Sync will resume when the service is restored."

## 8. Post-Launch Retrospective Template

### Schedule: T+7 (One Week After Launch)

### Agenda

1. **Metrics review** — Downloads, page views, conversion, social engagement
2. **What went well** — Celebrate wins explicitly
3. **What didn't go well** — Honest assessment, no blame
4. **What we'd change** — Specific improvements for next launch
5. **User feedback themes** — Top positive and negative feedback
6. **Press coverage assessment** — What landed, what didn't
7. **Community response** — HN, Reddit, Product Hunt analysis
8. **Action items** — Concrete next steps with owners

### Key Questions

- Did we hit our launch day success criteria?
- Which channel drove the most downloads?
- What was the most common user feedback?
- Were there any surprises (positive or negative)?
- Did the contingency plans get used? Were they adequate?
- What should we invest more in for the next major release?
- What should we stop doing?

### Retrospective Output

- Summary document shared with full team
- Action items tracked as GitHub issues
- Lessons learned incorporated into next launch playbook
- Metrics baseline established for future comparison
